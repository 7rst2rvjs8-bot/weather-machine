01  /  The idea

Forecasting got accurate by being wrong on the record.

Early models were crude. What made them better was discipline: each one logged exactly where it missed, then added a single correction term to catch that miss — and checked whether the term held on weather that hadn't happened yet. This system is built the same way. The goal isn't one clever model; it's a book of independent strategies, each running its own error-correction loop, with a meta-layer that moves capital toward what survives and retires what doesn't.

A correction term earns its place only by catching a real, named miss in data the system has never seen. Build all the plumbing you want — make no decision against old data.

The governing rule · what separates a weather machine from a curve-fit
02  /  The loop · one error-correction cycle

Every strategy is the same five-step loop, turned over and over.

01 · TRADE

Take the position

Live, or shadowed — a signal acted on, or logged as one that would have been.

02 · LOG THE MISS

Record where it was wrong

The miss is the fuel. Nothing improves without an honest record of the failure.

03 · PROPOSE THE TERM

One correction

The single term that would have caught that named miss — a fresh hypothesis, not a tweak.

04 · FORWARD-TEST

On unseen days

Shadow it forward against data it has never touched. Never rescued by re-reading the past.

05 · PROMOTE / KILL

Earned, or retired

Cheap to kill, hard to promote. A term survives only provisionally; a failure kills it definitively.

[↻]Repeat. The loop cannot turn faster than real market days accrue — the binding constraint is the calendar, not effort.
03  /  The machine

An instrument first. The edge is a separate question.

Most of the work so far has gone into proving the harness — the execution, logging, and test scaffolding — is trustworthy, kept strictly apart from whether the edge exists. A clean instrument can still measure an edge that isn't there. Conflating the two is the failure the project is built to avoid.

04  /  What's been measured

Three foundational assumptions, tested. None flattered the plan.

Before building further, three load-bearing assumptions were checked against existing data. They came back as findings — and each reshaped the roadmap rather than confirming it.

Does the champion have a real edge?
PLAUSIBLE — NOT CONFIRMED. Survives selection-deflation to roughly $4–5 per trade, with three larger doubts still open.
Is the fill model roughly right?
LATENCY IS A THREAT. Entry cost is on the order of the per-trade edge — lethal at low premium, which points to a premium floor.
Are five live streams five strategies?
NO — ABOUT 2.3 REAL BETS. Variants of one signal add almost no independence; genuine diversity needs genuinely different signals.

// the calendar finding: proving a single correction term takes hundreds of forward signals — months — not fifty. So the ladder graduates early and kills early, rather than waiting a fixed term.
