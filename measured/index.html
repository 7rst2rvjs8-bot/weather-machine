What's measured · the foundations

Three assumptions, tested.

Before building further, three load-bearing assumptions were checked against existing data. They came back as findings — and each reshaped the roadmap rather than confirming it. Stated here at the honest limit of what's actually known, not the flattering edge.

01  /  The findings
Does the champion have a real edge?
PLAUSIBLE — NOT CONFIRMED. Survives selection-deflation to roughly $4–5 per trade — but that figure is an upper bound. It only rules out final-stage selection among the candidates; three larger doubts stay open: parameter-tuning inside each candidate, a point-in-time universe-selection lookahead, and the finite mining budget. Not-falsified is not confirmed.
Is the fill model roughly right?
LATENCY IS A THREAT — AND BEING RE-MEASURED. An early latency estimate turned out to be largely a measurement artifact (a polling-cadence effect, not true reaction lag), so the latency conclusions are currently suspended pending a clean recompute. What's structural and survives: a roughly fixed entry cost is devastating at low premium and minor at high — pointing toward a premium floor as a refinement, not an invalidation.
Are five live streams five strategies?
NO — ABOUT 2.3 REAL BETS. Variants of one signal add almost no independence; the "five streams" are roughly two-to-three real bets in costumes. Genuine diversity needs genuinely different signals, instruments, or horizons — not more exit variants.

// A method note, logged: one selection check tested its own distributional assumption rather than assuming fat tails — the next one will do the same. The discipline applies to the measurements too.

02  /  The calendar finding

Proving one correction term costs months, not weeks.

At the current signal rate, validating a single few-dollar-per-trade term to adequate statistical power needs hundreds to roughly two thousand forward signals — on the order of seven months to a year and a half of real accrual, per term. That calendar price is why the ladder is built to graduate early and kill early on accumulating evidence, rather than waiting out a fixed term.
