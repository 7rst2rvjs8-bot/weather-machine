Findings · the foundations

Tested before building on them.

Three load-bearing assumptions were measured against existing data. Each returned a finding — and each reshaped the design rather than confirming it. Stated at the honest limit of what is known.

01 / The findings
Does the champion's edge survive scrutiny?
A FEW DOLLARS PER TRADE — A CONSERVATIVE BOUND. Edge — the average per-trade advantage after costs — survives selection-deflation to a few dollars. Three sources of optimism remain explicitly bounded, not hidden: parameter choice inside each candidate, a point-in-time universe-selection effect, and the finite comparison budget. Validation continues forward, out-of-sample.
Is the fill model right?
LATENCY IS THE BINDING EXECUTION RISK. A roughly fixed entry cost is negligible at high premium and decisive at low — which sets a premium floor on tradeable entries. The first estimate traced to a polling-cadence artifact; the measurement is being re-run against the real reaction band.
Are five live streams five strategies?
EFFECTIVE INDEPENDENCE ≈ 2.3 OF 5. Variants of one signal add almost no independent information. Genuine diversity requires different signals, instruments, or horizons — not more exit variants.

// A method note: one selection check tested its own distributional assumption rather than assuming it. The discipline applies to the measurements too.

02 / The calendar

One correction takes months to prove, not weeks.

At the current signal rate, validating a single few-dollar-per-trade term to adequate statistical power needs hundreds to a couple thousand forward observations — on the order of seven months to over a year per term. The ladder graduates early or kills early on accruing evidence, rather than waiting out a fixed term.
