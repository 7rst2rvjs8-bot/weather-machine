The findings

Does the champion's edge survive scrutiny?
A few dollars per trade — a conservative bound.

Edge — the average per-trade advantage after costs — survives selection-deflation to a few dollars. Three sources of optimism remain explicitly bounded, not hidden: parameter choice inside each candidate, a point-in-time universe-selection effect, and the finite comparison budget. Validation continues forward, out-of-sample.

Etrue = Emeasured − ΔSselection − costs
Is the fill model right?
Latency is the binding execution risk.

A roughly fixed entry cost is negligible at high premium and decisive at low — which sets a premium floor on tradeable entries. The first estimate traced to a polling-cadence artifact in the data, and is being re-run against the real reaction band.

Are five live streams five strategies?
Effective independence ≈ 2.3 of 5.

Variants of one signal add almost no independent information. Genuine diversity requires different signals, instruments, or horizons — not more exit variants.

// A method note:

One selection check tested its own distributional assumption rather than assuming it. The discipline applies to the measurements too.

02 /

The calendar

At the current signal rate, validating a single few-dollar-per-trade term to adequate statistical power needs hundreds to a couple thousand forward observations — on the order of seven months to over a year per term. The ladder graduates early or kills early on accruing evidence, rather than waiting out a fixed term.
