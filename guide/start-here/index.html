The whole idea, in a paragraph.

Weather forecasts didn't become accurate through a single brilliant model. They improved over decades by recording exactly where each forecast went wrong and adding one fix for that specific mistake, again and again. This is a trading system built the same way: it keeps a careful record of its own misses and earns one correction at a time.

Two rules keep it honest. A fix only counts if it works on days the system has never seen — not on the history it was tuned against. And no real money moves until the case for it is made on that fresh evidence. So far, the careful part is built and the trading has barely begun, which is exactly the intended order.

Everything else on the site is a closer look at one piece of that: how the loop works, what's been measured, what's certified, and what comes next.
