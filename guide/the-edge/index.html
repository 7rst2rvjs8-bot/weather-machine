Field guide · the basics

The edge.

“Edge” is one word for a plain question: does this make money on average, after costs? Everything else is about answering it without fooling anyone.

The trap is that a strategy can look excellent on past data purely by luck. Test enough variations against the same history and one of them will appear brilliant — the way the best-looking coin in a thousand flips looks special until you flip it again. A backtest is a hypothesis, never a verdict.

How the question gets answered

The edge is validated forward — on live market days the strategy has never seen — before real money scales behind it. Results on fresh data are the only ones that count.

Measured this way, the early signs are modest and stated conservatively: a few dollars of advantage per trade, with the remaining sources of optimism named and bounded rather than hidden. Whether that survives a long run of forward evidence is the work in progress.
