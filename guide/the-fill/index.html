Field guide · the basics

The fill.

A fill is a trade actually executing in the live market — the moment an intention becomes a real position at a real price.

Backtests assume that intention and execution are the same thing: the model wants to buy at a price, so it buys at that price. Reality charges a toll. By the time an order reaches the market the price has often moved, and at small option premiums that gap can swallow the entire advantage. No amount of historical testing measures it — only a real trade does.

Why one contract matters

The first fill is a single contract, placed by hand and watched the whole time. It answers one question nothing else can: does the real fill match the model? It settles nothing about whether the strategy earns — it measures the toll, once, for real.

That single data point is why the careful record-keeping has to exist first. A real fill that nobody captured in full is a measurement wasted.
