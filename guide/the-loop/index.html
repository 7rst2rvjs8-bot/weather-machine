Field guide · the basics

The correction loop.

The whole system is one loop, turned over and over: trade, record the mistake, propose the one fix that would have caught it, test that fix on fresh data, then keep it or kill it.

The mistake is the valuable part. A miss that's recorded in enough detail shows what kind of signal would have caught it — and that becomes the next thing to try. So the record of failures, kept honestly, is the fuel the whole engine runs on.

Why it's slow on purpose

A fix only earns its place by working on days the system has never seen. That takes real time to accumulate — there is no way to rush fresh evidence, and no shortcut through the past. The loop turns at the speed of the calendar.

Done this way, the system is meant to get steadily more capable without ever quietly tuning itself to look good on history it has already seen.
