Field guide · the basics

Shadow streams.

A shadow stream runs a strategy live during market hours and records every trade it would have made — without placing any of them.

It is the middle ground between a backtest and real trading. A backtest replays old data, which a strategy can be quietly tuned to flatter. Real trading risks money. A shadow stream takes the live market as it happens — the same prices, the same timing — and logs what the strategy decides, so the evidence accrues forward without a dollar exposed.

Why it matters

Forward evidence is the only kind that proves a strategy wasn't just fitted to the past. Shadow streams generate it cheaply, which is what lets candidate ideas compete before any of them earns real capital.

A signal that fires cleanly but couldn't actually be traded — too thin a market, too wide a spread — shows up here as a finding, not a silent failure. That is information, and it is kept.
